We can exemplify our proposed approach using a real scenario of collecting the census data of a nation. Let us consider a case of a country like India where the Govt. of India wants to keep a record of the entire population. As India is a large country comprising of 29 states and 7 union territories. These states and the union territories are divided into districts. At the time of Census 2001, there were in all 593 districts against 467 districts at the time of 1991 Census. Each district is further divided into sub-districts, which are known differently in different parts in the country (e.g., tahsil, taluka, community development (CD) block, Police station, Mandal, revenue circle, etc.). We can see a very large amount of data will be generated (Big Data) during a survey of collecting and analyzing the population of India. So, it will be a very strenuous job for a single organization to collect these Big Data in given time duration.

Our proposed system can handle Big Data securely and efficiently.
